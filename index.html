Project 001 is the opening move in a full 8-letter Inland Empire GPS-art route package. The current U import is live, and the master draft now maps the whole “U MAD BRO?” phrase west-to-east at roughly 80 miles with connectors.

Route Concept Console

The full master draft now defines a first-pass coordinate framework for all eight characters as one GPS-art project: U, M, A, D, B, R, O, and the final question mark. These are control vertices for route building, designed to be imported as separate GPX files or stitched into a full horizontal meme ultra.

The joke stays intact, but the framework is now concrete: 73.2 letter-miles, about 6.9 connector-miles, and a buildable first-pass package called U MAD BRO? — Inland Empire 80.

Build notes

8-Week Build

Weekly rollout Week 1 U, Week 2 M, Week 3 A, Week 4 D, Week 5 B, Week 6 R, Week 7 O, Week 8 question mark.
Geometry polish Keep overdraw where it matters: M center valley, B upper loop, R diagonal leg, and the question mark hook.
Field verification Before running, verify access, crossings, sidewalks, trail gates, and elevation spikes, then refine each letter in Strava Route Builder.
